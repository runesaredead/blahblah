Privacy Policy for crossXbot Chrome Extension

Last updated:

Thank you for installing crossXbot (the "Extension"). Your privacy is extremely important to us. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use that information, and the choices you have with respect to your information when you use the Extension.

1. Information We Collect

  1. OAuth Access Tokens
    • When you choose to connect your Twitter, Facebook, or Instagram accounts, we request temporary OAuth access tokens that allow the Extension to post content on your behalf.
    • Tokens are stored locally in your browser's storage (chrome.storage.local) and are never transmitted to, nor stored on, our servers.
  2. Post Content
    • When you press the "Tweet" button, the tweet text (and any attached media metadata) is made available to the Extension so that you can choose to cross-post it to other platforms.
    • The content is only used for immediate posting to the social networks you explicitly select and is not retained by us after the posting operation completes.
  3. Diagnostic Logs (Optional)
    • With your explicit consent, the Extension may log non-personally-identifiable information (e.g., success/failure codes) to help us diagnose issues. This data is stored locally and is only shared if you manually choose to send it to our support team.

The Extension does not collect your browsing history, form entries, passwords, or any other personal data beyond what is described above.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect solely to:

  • Authenticate your social-media accounts so that you may cross-post content.
  • Publish the content you explicitly choose to share on the selected social networks.
  • Provide you with real-time status (e.g., whether you are authenticated with Facebook or Instagram).
  • Improve functionality and troubleshoot bugs (if you have opted-in to diagnostic logging).

We never sell, rent, or otherwise monetize your information.

3. Data Storage & Security

  • All tokens and cached data are stored using Chrome's chrome.storage.local API, which keeps the data on your local device. If Chrome Sync is enabled, Google may encrypt and synchronize this data across your devices; you can disable Chrome Sync in your browser settings if preferred.
  • We employ industry-standard security practices—such as using HTTPS when communicating with Facebook and Instagram APIs—to protect the integrity and confidentiality of your data in transit.
  • We do not operate external servers that hold your personal information.

4. Data Retention

  • OAuth tokens remain in local storage until you revoke access, log out via the Extension, uninstall the Extension, or clear your browser data.
  • Diagnostic logs (if enabled) are retained for up to 30 days locally unless deleted sooner by you.
  • No other personal data is stored by the Extension.

5. Your Choices & Controls

  • Revoke Permissions – You can disconnect any social-media account at any time through the Extension's settings or via your social-network's app settings. This will invalidate the stored token.
  • Clear Stored Data – You may delete all locally stored data by uninstalling the Extension or clearing site data for the Extension in Chrome.
  • Uninstall – Removing the Extension immediately deletes all locally stored data associated with it.

6. Third-Party Services

The Extension interacts with the following third-party platforms solely to enable posting functionality:

Service Purpose Link to Privacy Policy

These services have their own privacy practices which we do not control; please review their policies.

6.1. Google APIs Limited Use Disclosure

Our use of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the Chrome Web Store User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

7. Children's Privacy

The Extension is not directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe we have inadvertently collected such information, please contact us so we can promptly delete it.

8. International Users & Legal Bases (GDPR / CCPA)

Depending on your location, you may have additional rights under applicable data-protection laws, including the right to access, correct, delete, or restrict the processing of your personal data. We process your data only with your consent (Art. 6 (1)(a) GDPR) and solely for the purpose of performing the contract of providing the Extension's services (Art. 6 (1)(b) GDPR).

We do not share data with third parties for direct marketing purposes (Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.83).

9. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Last updated" date at the top. Material changes will be communicated through the Chrome Web Store listing or via an in-extension notification so you can review them.
